## Design Context

### Users
Collectors and high-end buyers interested in physical copper bars as a commodity or material object. They visit on desktop. The experience is singular — one product, seen fully before any purchase intent. Context: quiet, deliberate browsing, not a marketplace.

### Brand Personality
Elemental. Permanent. Restrained. The copper bar is not a lifestyle product — it is a material fact. The brand voice is that of a refinery or assay house, not a retailer.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark luxury, not tech-dark. The background (#0A0807) reads as almost-black charcoal. Copper accent color is warm and rare, used sparingly. Typography is editorial-serif-led. Grain overlay adds tactility. This is closer to a museum placard or a commodity exchange certificate than an e-commerce page. Anti-reference: anything with gradients, glow, neon, or card grids.

### Design Principles
1. **Restraint as luxury** — every element earns its presence. If it can be removed, it should be.
2. **Material honesty** — the interface reflects the physicality of copper: weight, warmth, permanence.
3. **Scroll as choreography** — the hero animation is the product reveal. It should feel unhurried and inevitable.
4. **Silence before sound** — large dark spaces are not emptiness, they are weight.
5. **One thing at a time** — no competing focal points. Each scroll step resolves before the next begins.
