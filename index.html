Element 29

Copper has been worked by human hands for more than ten thousand years. It was the first metal we shaped into tools, the first we cast, the first we alloyed. Before iron. Before steel. Copper was civilisation's beginning.

Of all conductive metals, copper carries current with the least resistance. It moves heat, electricity, and signal with a purity that no synthetic material has surpassed. The physical world still depends on it.

A refined copper bar is the material in its most essential form — extracted, purified, cast, and finished. No alloy. No coating. Nothing added. Copper as an idea made solid.
